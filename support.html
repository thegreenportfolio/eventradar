Event Radar – Support

Need help? We’re here for you.

Event Radar helps you search for public events by country, city, category, and date range. You can save selected events locally on your device and optionally add them to Apple Calendar. No account or sign-in is required.

❓ FAQ

Q: Does Event Radar require an account?

A: No. Event Radar does not require an account, sign-in, username, or password. You can search events and save selected events locally on your device.

Q: What does Event Radar do?

A: Event Radar helps you search for public events in selected cities. You can choose a country, city, category, and date range, then review event results, save selected events, open event websites, or add saved events to Apple Calendar.

Q: What event categories can I search?

A: Event categories may include concerts, sports, festivals, fairs, arts, shows, and other public events depending on availability in the selected city.

Q: Why do some cities show fewer events?

A: Event results depend on third-party event sources and public event listings. Some cities, categories, or date ranges may have more coverage than others.

Q: Are saved events stored online?

A: No. Saved events are stored locally on your device. Event Radar does not require an online account or cloud profile.

Q: Can I delete saved events?

A: Yes. Saved events can be removed from within the app where available. You can also remove local app data by deleting the app from your device.

Q: Why does Event Radar ask for Calendar access?

A: Calendar access is used only when you choose to add a saved event to Apple Calendar. You can manage calendar permission at any time in your device Settings.

Q: Does Event Radar sell my data?

A: No. Event Radar does not sell your personal data and does not use advertising trackers for user profiling.

Q: Are event details always accurate?

A: Event information comes from third-party event sources and public listings. Dates, times, venues, ticket availability, prices, and cancellation status may change. Always confirm details with the official organizer, venue, or ticket provider before attending or purchasing tickets.

Q: What happens when I open an event website?

A: Some events include external website links. These websites are controlled by third parties and may have their own privacy policies, ticket rules, prices, and terms.

📅 Calendar Help

To add an event to Apple Calendar, save the event first, open your saved events, and choose the calendar option where available.

If calendar access does not work, check your device settings:

  • Open iPhone Settings.
  • Find Event Radar.
  • Allow Calendar access.

🛡️ Safety

Event Radar helps you discover events, but you are responsible for making safe decisions when attending any event.

  • Confirm event details before going.
  • Use official ticket providers and trusted websites.
  • Be careful when visiting unfamiliar locations.
  • Follow local laws, venue rules, and safety instructions.
  • Do not share personal information with unknown third parties.
